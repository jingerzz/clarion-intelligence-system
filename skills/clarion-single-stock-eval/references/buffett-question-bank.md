# Buffett's question bank

Four lenses from Berkshire Hathaway annual letters and shareholder Q&As. Apply them to the script's filing snippets to produce an evaluation.

## 1. Moat

- What is this business's competitive advantage?
- Is the advantage durable — would it survive a price war, a downturn, or a new entrant with deep pockets?
- Is it widening or narrowing?
- Does the company describe its moat in **specific** terms (named customers, multi-year contracts, switching costs, scale economics, brand equity, IP) — or in vague marketing language?

A real moat appears in MD&A and Item 1 Business with concrete numbers and named details. "Strong brand" in isolation isn't a moat; "brand recognition above 80% in seven core markets, supported by $X billion in advertising spend over Y years" is one.

## 2. Management

- Are leaders **aligned** with long-term shareholders (skin in the game, comp tied to long-term metrics — not just stock price)?
- How do they **allocate capital** — buybacks at attractive prices, dividends, M&A discipline, organic reinvestment, debt paydown?
- Do they communicate **honestly** in MD&A — do they tell you when results disappointed, why, and what they're doing about it?
- Have they **hit prior guidance**, or revised targets down repeatedly?

Look for: explicit discussion of buyback timing relative to intrinsic value; segment-level capital allocation; management retention; insider ownership %; restraint in M&A premium paid.

## 3. Financial trends

- Is **revenue** growing, stable, or contracting? At what rate vs the industry?
- Are **operating and gross margins** expanding or eroding? Why?
- Is **free cash flow** healthy and growing — and is it FCF, or is it propped up by working capital releases, asset sales, or stock-based comp?
- What's the **leverage profile**? Is debt service comfortable through a downturn (interest coverage, covenant headroom)?

A "good" trend is multi-year and supported by the financial statements footnotes, not a one-quarter beat.

## 4. Risks

- What's the **most severe** risk in the indexed filings (highest severity in the lens output)?
- Is it **tail** (unlikely but ruinous — major litigation, regulatory ban, key customer loss) or **recurring** (drag on returns — commodity cycles, FX exposure, technology cycle)?
- Is the company **mitigating** the top risks — diversification, hedging, regulatory engagement, balance sheet strength?
- Are there risks the company is **not** disclosing that you'd want answered? (Note: this is where the limits of pure-filing analysis show; you may need primary research beyond the 10-K.)

## Synthesis: the Buffett evaluation

After running the four lenses, write a brief that answers:

1. **What I believe** — one paragraph, plain English.
2. **Why I believe it** — numbered evidence drawn from filings (cite each).
3. **What it's worth** — bear / base / bull scenarios with rough numbers (margin range × revenue range × multiple range, then sanity-check against current price).
4. **What changes my mind** — kill conditions: specific, measurable, falsifiable.
5. **Why now** — the catalyst, or the patience case for waiting.

The script provides the raw data. You provide the thesis.

A good evaluation is concise — under 800 words is plenty. If you need more, the thesis isn't clear enough yet.
